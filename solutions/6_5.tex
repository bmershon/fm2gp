\section*{6.5}

\textit{Solution.}

The element $a$ has order $n$, which means $a^{n-1}$ applications of $a$ to the element $a$ produces $e$.

$$a \cdot a^{n-1} = e = a \cdot a^{-1} = a^{-1} \cdot a$$

By the cancellation axiom for a group, this implies that $a^{-1} = a^{n-1}$.
